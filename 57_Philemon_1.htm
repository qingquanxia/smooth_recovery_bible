THE EPISTLE OF PAUL TO PHILEMON

Pm 1Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus, and Timothy the brother, to Philemon our beloved and fellow worker

Pm 2and to Apphia the sister and to Archippus our fellow soldier and to the church, which is in your house:

Pm 3Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

Pm 4I thank my God always, making mention of you in my prayers,

Pm 5hearing of your love and the faith which you have toward the Lord Jesus and to all the saints,

Pm 6that the fellowship of your faith may become effective in the full knowledge of every good thing which is in you for Christ.

Pm 7For I had much joy and encouragement over your love, because the inward parts of the saints have been refreshed through you, brother.

Pm 8Therefore though I have much boldness in Christ to charge you what is fitting,

Pm 9because of love I rather entreat, being such a one as Paul the aged and now also a prisoner of Christ Jesus.

Pm 10I entreat you concerning my child, whom I have begotten in my bonds, Onesimus,

Pm 11who formerly was useless to you but now is useful both to you and to me.

Pm 12Him I have sent back to you — him, that is, my very heart —

Pm 13whom I intended to keep with myself that on your behalf he might minister to me in the bonds of the gospel.

Pm 14But without your mind I did not want to do anything, that your goodness would not be as of necessity, but voluntary.

Pm 15For perhaps for this reason he was separated from you for but an hour, that you might fully have him forever,

Pm 16no longer as a slave, but above a slave, a beloved brother, especially to me, but how much more to you, both in the flesh and in the Lord.

Pm 17If then you hold me as a partner, receive him as myself.

Pm 18And if he has wronged you in anything or owes anything, charge that to my account.

Pm 19I, Paul, have written with my own hand: I will repay; not to say to you that you owe me even your own self besides.

Pm 20Yes, brother, may I have profit from you in the Lord; refresh my inward parts in Christ.

Pm 21Having confidence in your obedience, I have written to you, knowing that you will do even beyond the things that I say.

Pm 22And at the same time also prepare me a lodging, for I hope that through your prayers I will be graciously given to you.

Pm 23Epaphras, my fellow prisoner in Christ Jesus, greets you;

Pm 24as do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, Luke, my fellow workers.

Pm 25The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.
