TITUS 3

vv. 123456789101112131415

Tt 3:1Remind them to be subject to rulers, to authorities, to be obedient, to be ready unto every good work,

Tt 3:2to slander no one, to be uncontentious, forbearing, showing all meekness toward all men.

Tt 3:3For we also were once foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving as slaves various lusts and pleasures, spending our lives in malice and envy, hateful, hating one another.

Tt 3:4But when the kindness and the love to man of our Savior God appeared,

Tt 3:5not out of works in righteousness which we did but according to His mercy He saved us, through the washing of regeneration and the renewing of the Holy Spirit,

Tt 3:6whom He poured out upon us richly through Jesus Christ our Savior,

Tt 3:7in order that having been justified by His grace, we might become heirs according to the hope of eternal life.

Tt 3:8Faithful is the word, and concerning these things I desire you to confidently affirm, so that those who have believed in God may be careful to maintain respectable works. These things are good and profitable to men.

Tt 3:9But avoid foolish questionings and genealogies and strifes and contentions about the law, for they are unprofitable and vain.

Tt 3:10A factious man, after a first and second admonition, refuse,

Tt 3:11knowing that such a one is perverted and is sinning, though he is condemned by his own self.

Tt 3:12When I send Artemas to you or Tychicus, be diligent to come to me at Nicopolis, for I have decided to spend the winter there.

Tt 3:13Zenas the lawyer and Apollos send forward diligently that nothing may be lacking to them.

Tt 3:14And let those also who are ours learn to maintain respectable works for their necessary needs, that they may not be unfruitful.

Tt 3:15All who are with me greet you. Greet those who love us in faith. Grace be with you all.
