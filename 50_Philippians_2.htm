Pp 2:1If there is therefore any encouragement in Christ, if any consolation of love, if any fellowship of spirit, if any tenderheartedness and compassions,

Pp 2:2make my joy full, that you think the same thing, having the same love, joined in soul, thinking the one thing,

Pp 2:3doing nothing by way of selfish ambition nor by way of vainglory, but in lowliness of mind considering one another more excellent than yourselves;

Pp 2:4not regarding each his own virtues, but each the virtues of others also.

Pp 2:5Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus,

Pp 2:6who, existing in the form of God, did not consider being equal with God a treasure to be grasped,

Pp 2:7but emptied Himself, taking the form of a slave, becoming in the likeness of men;

Pp 2:8and being found in fashion as a man, He humbled Himself, becoming obedient even unto death, and that the death of a cross.

Pp 2:9Therefore also God highly exalted Him and bestowed on Him the name which is above every name,

Pp 2:10that in the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those who are in heaven and on earth and under the earth,

Pp 2:11and every tongue should openly confess that Jesus Christ is Lord to the glory of God the Father.

Pp 2:12So then, my beloved, even as you have always obeyed, not as in my presence only but now much rather in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling;

Pp 2:13for it is God who operates in you both the willing and the working for His good pleasure.

Pp 2:14Do all things without murmurings and reasonings

Pp 2:15that you may be blameless and guileless, children of God without blemish in the midst of a crooked and perverted generation, among whom you shine as luminaries in the world,

Pp 2:16holding forth the word of life, so that I may have a boast in the day of Christ that I did not run in vain nor labor in vain.

Pp 2:17But even if I am being poured out as a drink offering upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice, and I rejoice together with you all.

Pp 2:18And in like manner you also rejoice, and you rejoice together with me.

Pp 2:19But I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy to you shortly, that I also may be encouraged by knowing the things concerning you.

Pp 2:20For I have no one like-souled who will genuinely care for what concerns you;

Pp 2:21for all seek their own things, not the things of Christ Jesus.

Pp 2:22But you know his approvedness, that as a child with a father he has served with me unto the gospel.

Pp 2:23Him therefore I hope to send immediately, as soon as I see how things go with me.

Pp 2:24But I trust in the Lord that I myself also will come shortly.

Pp 2:25But I considered it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother and fellow worker and fellow soldier, and your apostle and minister to my need,

Pp 2:26since he longed after you all and was greatly distressed because you heard that he had become sick;

Pp 2:27for indeed he had become sick, near to death, but God had mercy on him, and not on him only but also on me that I might not have sorrow upon sorrow.

Pp 2:28I have sent him therefore the more eagerly, so that when you see him again, you may rejoice and I may be less sorrowful.

Pp 2:29Receive him therefore in the Lord with all joy, and hold such in honor,

Pp 2:30because for the work of Christ he drew near unto death, risking his life, that he might fill up your lack of service toward me.
