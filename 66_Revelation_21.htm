REVELATION 21

vv. 123456789101112131415161718192021222324252627

Rv 21:1And I saw a new heaven and a new earth; for the first heaven and the first earth passed away, and the sea is no more.

Rv 21:2And I saw the holy city, New Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, prepared as a bride adorned for her husband.

Rv 21:3And I heard a loud voice out of the throne, saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and He will tabernacle with them, and they will be His peoples, and God Himself will be with them and be their God.

Rv 21:4And He will wipe away every tear from their eyes; and death will be no more, nor will there be sorrow or crying or pain anymore; for the former things have passed away.

Rv 21:5And He who sits on the throne said, Behold, I make all things new. And He said, Write, for these words are faithful and true.

Rv 21:6And He said to me, They have come to pass. I am the Alpha and the Omega, the Beginning and the End. I will give to him who thirsts from the spring of the water of life freely.

Rv 21:7He who overcomes will inherit these things, and I will be God to him, and he will be a son to Me.

Rv 21:8But the cowardly and unbelieving and abominable and murderers and fornicators and sorcerers and idolaters and all the false, their part will be in the lake which burns with fire and brimstone, which is the second death.

Rv 21:9And one of the seven angels who had the seven bowls full of the seven last plagues came and spoke with me, saying, Come here; I will show you the bride, the wife of the Lamb.

Rv 21:10And he carried me away in spirit onto a great and high mountain and showed me the holy city, Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God,

Rv 21:11having the glory of God. Her light was like a most precious stone, like a jasper stone, as clear as crystal.

Rv 21:12It had a great and high wall and had twelve gates, and at the gates twelve angels, and names inscribed, which are the names of the twelve tribes of the sons of Israel:

Rv 21:13on the east three gates, and on the north three gates, and on the south three gates, and on the west three gates.

Rv 21:14And the wall of the city had twelve foundations, and on them the twelve names of the twelve apostles of the Lamb.

Rv 21:15And he who spoke with me had a golden reed as a measure that he might measure the city and its gates and its wall.

Rv 21:16And the city lies square, and its length is as great as the breadth. And he measured the city with the reed to a length of twelve thousand stadia; the length and the breadth and the height of it are equal.

Rv 21:17And he measured its wall, a hundred and forty-four cubits, according to the measure of a man, that is, of an angel.

Rv 21:18And the building work of its wall was jasper; and the city was pure gold, like clear glass.

Rv 21:19The foundations of the wall of the city were adorned with every precious stone: the first foundation was jasper; the second, sapphire; the third, chalcedony; the fourth, emerald;

Rv 21:20the fifth, sardonyx; the sixth, sardius; the seventh, chrysolite; the eighth, beryl; the ninth, topaz; the tenth, chrysoprase; the eleventh, jacinth; the twelfth, amethyst.

Rv 21:21And the twelve gates were twelve pearls; each one of the gates was, respectively, of one pearl. And the street of the city was pure gold, like transparent glass.

Rv 21:22And I saw no temple in it, for the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb are its temple.

Rv 21:23And the city has no need of the sun or of the moon that they should shine in it, for the glory of God illumined it, and its lamp is the Lamb.

Rv 21:24And the nations will walk by its light; and the kings of the earth bring their glory into it.

Rv 21:25And its gates shall by no means be shut by day, for there will be no night there.

Rv 21:26And they will bring the glory and the honor of the nations into it.

Rv 21:27And anything common and he who makes an abomination and a lie shall by no means enter into it, but only those who are written in the Lamb’s book of life.
