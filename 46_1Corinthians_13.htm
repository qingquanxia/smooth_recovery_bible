1 CORINTHIANS 13

vv. 12345678910111213

1Co 13:1If I speak in the tongues of men and of angels but do not have love, I have become sounding brass or a clanging cymbal.

1Co 13:2And if I have the gift of prophecy and know all mysteries and all knowledge, and if I have all faith so as to remove mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing.

1Co 13:3And if I dole out all my possessions to feed others, and if I deliver up my body that I may boast, but do not have love, I profit nothing.

1Co 13:4Love suffers long. Love is kind; it is not jealous. Love does not brag and is not puffed up;

1Co 13:5it does not behave unbecomingly and does not seek its own things; it is not provoked and does not take account of evil;

1Co 13:6it does not rejoice because of unrighteousness, but rejoices with the truth;

1Co 13:7it covers all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.

1Co 13:8Love never falls away. But whether prophecies, they will be rendered useless; or tongues, they will cease; or knowledge, it will be rendered useless.

1Co 13:9For we know in part, and we prophesy in part;

1Co 13:10but when that which is complete comes, that which is in part will be rendered useless.

1Co 13:11When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I thought as a child, I reasoned as a child; since I have become a man, I have done away with childish things.

1Co 13:12For now we see in a mirror obscurely, but at that time face to face; now I know in part, but at that time I will fully know even as also I was fully known.

1Co 13:13Now there abide faith, hope, love, these three; and the greatest of these is love.
